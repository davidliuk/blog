    1

    Given a rotation angle about the x-axis, I showed in class how to derive the associated rotation matrix

    R10(x,ϕ)=RX=(1000cos⁡ϕ−sin⁡ϕ0sin⁡ϕcos⁡ϕ) R_1^0(x,\phi)=R_X=\begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & \cos\phi & -\sin\phi \\ 0 & \sin\phi & \cos\phi \end{pmatrix} R10​(x,ϕ)=RX​=​100​0cosϕsinϕ​0−sinϕcosϕ​​

    representing how vectors specified in the rotated (1) coordinate system can be represented in the unrotated (0) coordinate system. Using a similar approach, show that:

    R10(z,ψ)=RZ=(cos⁡ψ−sin⁡ψ0sin⁡ψcos⁡ψ0001) R_1^0(z,\psi)=R_Z = \begin{pmatrix} \cos\psi & -\sin\psi & 0 \\ \sin\psi & \cos\psi & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix} R10​(z,ψ)=RZ​=​cosψsinψ0​−sinψcosψ0​001​​

    Answer:

    20-5, The columns of the matrices correspond to the axes. Your sin should be negative in your y component but in the i hat direction not in your x component.

    Let p⃗\vec pp​ represent the vector

    p⃗=px0i^0+py0j^0+pz0k^0p⃗=px1i^1+py1j^1+pz1k^1 \vec p=p^0_x\hat i_0+p^0_y\hat j_0+p^0_z\hat k_0\\ \vec p=p^1_x\hat i_1+p^1_y\hat j_1+p^1_z\hat k_1 p​=px0​i^0​+py0​j^​0​+pz0​k^0​p​=px1​i^1​+py1​j^​1​+pz1​k^1​

    We want to find the equivalent vector p⃗\vec pp​ represnted in the System 1

    px0i^0=px0cos⁡ψi^1−px0sin⁡ψj^1py0j^0=py0sin⁡ψi^1+py0cos⁡ψj^1pz0k^0=pz0k^1⇒(i^0j^0k^0)=(cos⁡ψ−sin⁡ψ0sin⁡ψcos⁡ψ0001)(i^1j^1k^1) \begin{equation} \begin{aligned} p_x^0\hat i_0&=p_x^0\cos\psi\hat i_1-p_x^0\sin\psi\hat j_1&\\ p_y^0\hat j_0&=p_y^0\sin\psi\hat i_1+p_y^0\cos\psi\hat j_1\\ p_z^0\hat k_0&=p_z^0\hat k_1 \end{aligned}\\ \Rightarrow \begin{pmatrix} \hat i_0\\\hat j_0\\\hat k_0 \end{pmatrix}=\begin{pmatrix} \cos\psi & -\sin\psi & 0 \\ \sin\psi & \cos\psi & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} \hat i_1\\\hat j_1\\\hat k_1 \end{pmatrix} \end{equation} px0​i^0​py0​j^​0​pz0​k^0​​=px0​cosψi^1​−px0​sinψj^​1​=py0​sinψi^1​+py0​cosψj^​1​=pz0​k^1​​⇒​i^0​j^​0​k^0​​​=​cosψsinψ0​−sinψcosψ0​001​​​i^1​j^​1​k^1​​​​​

    So we can get the result:

    p⃗0=R10(z,ψ)p⃗1R10(z,ψ)=RZ=(cos⁡ψ−sin⁡ψ0sin⁡ψcos⁡ψ0001) \vec p^0=R_1^0(z,\psi)\vec p^1\\ R_1^0(z,\psi)=R_Z = \begin{pmatrix} \cos\psi & -\sin\psi & 0 \\ \sin\psi & \cos\psi & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix} p​0=R10​(z,ψ)p​1R10​(z,ψ)=RZ​=​cosψsinψ0​−sinψcosψ0​001​​

That is, the order of rotation corresponds to ZYX.

    b) (5pts) Does the rotation matrix RZYXR_{ZYX}RZYX​ you have derived transform vectors from the unrotated frame to the rotated frame, or from the rotated frame to the unrotated frame? How do you know?
