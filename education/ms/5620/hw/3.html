    **b) **

    i) What do the vectors bjx,bjy,bjz\mathbf{b}_{jx}, \mathbf{b}_{jy}, \mathbf{b}_{jz}bjx​,bjy​,bjz​ represent for joint j?

    The vectors bjx,bjy,bjz\mathbf{b}_{jx}, \mathbf{b}_{jy}, \mathbf{b}_{jz}bjx​,bjy​,bjz​ represent the contribution of joint jjj's rotation to the end-effector's velocity along the x, y, and z axes, respectively. These are derived from the cross product of the unit vectors along the local axes of joint jjj and the radius vector from joint jjj to the end-effector nnn. More precisely:

    • bjx=a^jx×rjn\mathbf{b}_{jx} = \hat{a}_{jx} \times \mathbf{r}_{jn}bjx​=a^jx​×rjn​
    • bjy=a^jy×rjn\mathbf{b}_{jy} = \hat{a}_{jy} \times \mathbf{r}_{jn}bjy​=a^jy​×rjn​
    • bjz=a^jz×rjn\mathbf{b}_{jz} = \hat{a}_{jz} \times \mathbf{r}_{jn}bjz​=a^jz​×rjn​

    Where a^jx,a^jy,a^jz\hat{a}_{jx}, \hat{a}_{jy}, \hat{a}_{jz}a^jx​,a^jy​,a^jz​ are the unit vectors of the local axes of joint jjj, and rjn\mathbf{r}_{jn}rjn​ is the vector from joint jjj to the end-effector (right wrist, joint 9).

    These vectors represent the instantaneous linear velocity directions at the end effector caused by rotation about the x, y, and z axes of joint j respectively.

    The Jacobian matrix JJJ relates the end-effector's velocity to the joint velocities. To compute the Jacobian, we need to consider how each joint in the chain contributes to the velocity of the rWrist (joint 9). For each joint jjj, the contribution is given by:

    vn=JΘ˙=∑j=19(bjxθ˙jx+bjyθ˙jy+bjzθ˙jz)\mathbf{v}_n = J \dot{\Theta} = \sum_{j=1}^{9} (\mathbf{b}_{jx} \dot{\theta}_{jx} + \mathbf{b}_{jy} \dot{\theta}_{jy} + \mathbf{b}_{jz} \dot{\theta}_{jz})vn​=JΘ˙=∑j=19​(bjx​θ˙jx​+bjy​θ˙jy​+bjz​θ˙jz​)

    Thus, the total velocity of the rWrist is the sum of the contributions from the joints in the chain. Each joint contributes based on its rotation and its distance from the end-effector.

    The minimal set of equations needed to compute the Jacobian is:

    1. Compute the rotation matrices Rj0R^0_jRj0​ for each joint in the chain (joints 1 to 9).
    2. Compute the radius vectors rjn\mathbf{r}_{jn}rjn​ for each joint (from joint j to joint 9).
    3. Compute the cross products bjx,bjy,bjz\mathbf{b}_{jx}, \mathbf{b}_{jy}, \mathbf{b}_{jz}bjx​,bjy​,bjz​ for each joint to form the submatrices of the Jacobian.

    The final Jacobian JJJ is a 3x9 matrix that relates the joint velocities Θ˙\dot{\Theta}Θ˙ to the velocity of the rWrist in world coordinates.
